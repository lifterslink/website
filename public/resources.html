Frequently Asked Questions

What equipment do I need for my first powerlifting meet? ▼
For your first meet, you'll need: an approved singlet, flat-soled shoes (or deadlift slippers), knee-high socks for deadlifts, and a belt (optional but recommended). Check your federation's approved equipment list for specific brand requirements.
How do weight classes work in powerlifting? ▼
Powerlifting competitions are divided into weight classes to ensure fair competition. IPF weight classes for men are: 59kg, 66kg, 74kg, 83kg, 93kg, 105kg, 120kg, 120kg+. For women: 47kg, 52kg, 57kg, 63kg, 69kg, 76kg, 84kg, 84kg+.
What's the difference between equipped and raw powerlifting? ▼
Raw powerlifting allows minimal equipment: belt, wrist wraps, and knee sleeves. Equipped powerlifting permits supportive gear like squat/deadlift suits and bench shirts that can significantly increase the weight lifted.
How do I qualify for nationals or international meets? ▼
Qualification standards vary by federation. Typically, you need to achieve a qualifying total at a sanctioned local or regional meet. Check your federation's website for specific qualifying totals for your weight class and division.
